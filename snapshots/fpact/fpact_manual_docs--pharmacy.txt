Family PACT – Pharmacy Formulary
Pharmacy Formulary
Page updated: October 2025
For a list of both prescription and over-the-counter drugs and contraceptive supplies that are
reimbursable for pharmacy dispensing through the Family Planning, Access, Care and
Treatment (Family PACT) Program, providers should refer to the Family PACT Pharmacy
Formulary. Guidelines for pharmacy and onsite dispensing may differ for some drugs.
Restrictions are noted throughout the Family PACT Pharmacy Formulary. The use of drugs
outside of the specified conditions is not reimbursable.
‹‹For specific coverage criteria for oral contraceptives and oral emergency contraceptives,
Family PACT defers to Medi-Cal coverage. Refer to the Contract Drugs & Covered Products
Lists page on the Medi-Cal Rx Web Portal for more information.
• For specific coverage criteria for oral contraceptives and ulipristal acetate emergency
contraceptive, refer to the Medi-Cal Rx Contract Drugs List.
• For specific coverage criteria for non-contracted oral contraceptives, refer to the
Medi-Cal Rx Approved NDC List.
• For specific coverage criteria for over-the-counter (OTC) oral contraceptives (such as
the norgestrel progestin-only pill) and levonorgestrel emergency contraceptives, refer
to the Medi-Cal Rx Contract Drugs List – Over-the-Counter Drugs and Cough/Cold
Preparations.››
Reimbursable regimens for the management of covered family planning-related conditions
are also listed in the Family PACT Pharmacy Formulary.
